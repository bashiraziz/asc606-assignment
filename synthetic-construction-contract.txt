ENGINEERING, PROCUREMENT AND CONSTRUCTION CONTRACT

Contract No.: EPC-2025-0112
Effective Date: February 1, 2026
Substantial Completion Date (Target): January 31, 2027

PARTIES

Contractor: Meridian Engineering Group Ltd., a UK registered company ("Contractor")
Owner:      Gulf Ports Authority, a statutory body ("Owner")

---

RECITALS

The Owner wishes to construct a bulk cargo handling facility at Port Rashid, including
civil works, mechanical installation, and commissioning. The Contractor has been selected
through competitive tender to deliver the works described in this Contract on a fixed-price,
lump-sum basis.

---

ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Works" means all engineering, procurement, construction, installation, testing,
    and commissioning activities necessary to deliver the facility as described in
    the Technical Specification (Appendix 1).

1.2 "Substantial Completion" means the point at which the facility passes the
    Performance Tests set out in Appendix 2 and is certified as ready for commercial
    operation by the Owner's Independent Engineer.

1.3 "Defects Liability Period" means the 12-month period following the Substantial
    Completion Date during which the Contractor is obligated to remedy defects
    at no additional cost to the Owner.

1.4 "Progress Certificate" means the monthly certificate issued by the Owner's
    Independent Engineer confirming costs incurred and percentage completion.

---

ARTICLE 2 — PERFORMANCE OBLIGATIONS

2.1  ENGINEERING, PROCUREMENT AND CONSTRUCTION (EPC WORKS)

     The Contractor shall design, procure, and construct the bulk cargo handling
     facility in full conformance with the Technical Specification.

     Contract Price (EPC Works): USD 48,000,000 (fixed lump sum)
     Contract Duration: February 1, 2026 through January 31, 2027 (12 months)

     Revenue on the EPC Works shall be measured using the input method based on
     costs incurred relative to total estimated costs at completion. The Contractor's
     quantity surveying team shall certify actual costs monthly. The Owner's Independent
     Engineer shall review and approve each Progress Certificate within 10 business days
     of submission.

     Estimated Cost at Completion: USD 38,400,000
     Estimated Gross Margin: 20%

     Monthly invoicing shall be based on approved Progress Certificates.
     Payment is due within 28 days of Owner approval.

2.2  COMMISSIONING AND PERFORMANCE TESTING MILESTONE

     Upon completion of construction, the Contractor shall commission the facility
     and conduct the Performance Tests described in Appendix 2.

     Milestone: Issuance of the Substantial Completion Certificate by the Owner's
     Independent Engineer, certifying that the facility has passed all Performance Tests
     and is ready for commercial operation.

     Milestone Fee: USD 4,000,000 (payable within 14 days of the Substantial
     Completion Certificate being issued)

     Revenue on this obligation shall be recognized at the point in time when the
     Substantial Completion Certificate is issued, as this represents the moment
     control of the commissioned and tested facility transfers to the Owner.

     The Milestone Fee is not contingent on the facility achieving any specific
     throughput rate during the Defects Liability Period.

ARTICLE 4 — STANDALONE SELLING PRICES

     EPC Works: USD 48,000,000 — consistent with the Contractor's observable
     tender pricing for comparable port infrastructure projects of similar scope
     and complexity in the Gulf region.

     Commissioning Milestone: USD 4,000,000 — consistent with the Contractor's
     standard commissioning fee schedule (approximately 8% of EPC contract value
     for facilities of this type).

     No reallocation is required as SSPs equal the contract price.

---

ARTICLE 5 — PAYMENT TERMS

5.1  EPC Works: monthly invoicing based on approved Progress Certificates.
     Payment due 28 days from Owner approval of each certificate.

5.2  Mobilisation advance: USD 4,800,000 (10% of EPC Works price) payable
     within 14 days of Contract execution, to be recovered ratably over the
     first 6 months of the contract via deduction from Progress Certificates.

5.3  Retention: 5% of each Progress Certificate payment shall be withheld by
     the Owner and released 50% at Substantial Completion and 50% at expiry
     of the Defects Liability Period.

5.4  Commissioning Milestone Fee: payable within 14 days of issuance of the
     Substantial Completion Certificate.

---

ARTICLE 6 — VARIATIONS

6.1  The Owner may instruct variations to the Works in writing. The Contractor
     shall price each variation within 10 business days of instruction.
6.2  Agreed variations increase or decrease the Contract Price accordingly.
6.3  Variations exceeding 15% of the original Contract Price require a formal
     contract amendment signed by both parties.

---

ARTICLE 7 — DELAY AND LIQUIDATED DAMAGES

7.1  If Substantial Completion is not achieved by January 31, 2027 (subject to
     agreed extensions of time), the Contractor shall pay liquidated damages of
     USD 50,000 per calendar day of delay, up to a cap of 10% of the Contract Price.

7.2  Extensions of time shall be granted for Owner-caused delays, force majeure
     events, or Owner-instructed variations that affect the critical path.

---

ARTICLE 8 — DEFECTS LIABILITY

8.1  The Defects Liability Period runs for 12 months from the Substantial
     Completion Date.
8.2  The Contractor shall remedy any defect notified by the Owner during this
     period within 30 days, at the Contractor's cost.
8.3  The remaining 50% of retention is released at expiry of the Defects
     Liability Period provided no outstanding defects remain.

---

ARTICLE 9 — GOVERNING LAW

This Contract is governed by the laws of England and Wales. Disputes shall
be referred to arbitration under ICC Rules, seat London.

APPENDIX 1 — SCOPE SUMMARY (Technical Specification Reference)

Bulk Cargo Handling Facility — Port Rashid
  - Quay length: 350 metres (2 berths)
  - Ship unloader: 2 × 1,500 t/hr continuous ship unloaders
  - Conveyor system: 4.2 km enclosed conveyor network
  - Storage: 2 × covered storage domes, capacity 80,000 MT each
  - Reclaim and loadout system to landside transport
  - Control room, utilities, and site infrastructure

---

APPENDIX 2 — PERFORMANCE TESTS (Summary)

The facility must demonstrate, over a continuous 72-hour test run:
  - Ship unloading rate: minimum 1,200 t/hr per unloader
  - Conveyor availability: minimum 95%
  - Storage integrity: no material ingress or moisture variance outside specification
  - Control system: all interlocks and alarms functional per design

Failure to meet any criterion requires remediation and retest before the
Substantial Completion Certificate can be issued.
