### Performance Obligation 1 — EPC Works

| Field | Expected Answer |
|---|---|
| **Obligation Name** | Engineering, Procurement and Construction of bulk cargo handling facility |
| **Recognition Method** | Cost-to-cost (input method) |
| **Allocated Transaction Price** | USD 48,000,000 |
| **Start Date** | February 1, 2026 |
| **End Date** | January 31, 2027 |

**Expected reasoning:** The EPC Works obligation is satisfied over time because the Owner controls the asset as it is constructed (it is being built on the Owner's land and integrated into existing port infrastructure — it has no alternative use to the Contractor), and the Contractor has an enforceable right to payment for performance to date (monthly Progress Certificates, Article 2.1). This satisfies ASC 606-10-25-27(b) and (c). The input method (cost-to-cost) is specified in the contract: "revenue shall be measured using the input method based on costs incurred relative to total estimated costs at completion." Monthly Progress Certificates approved by the Owner's Independent Engineer provide the cost data.

**Note for graders:** The contract is highly specific about the measurement method. Students who recognise over-time treatment but argue for time-elapsed (rather than cost-to-cost) are partially correct on the over-time determination but wrong on measurement — deduct on ASC 606 reasoning.

### Performance Obligation 2 — Commissioning and Performance Testing

| Field | Expected Answer |
|---|---|
| **Obligation Name** | Commissioning and Performance Testing — Substantial Completion Milestone |
| **Recognition Method** | Point-in-time |
| **Allocated Transaction Price** | USD 4,000,000 |
| **Start Date** | N/A (single event) |
| **End Date / Trigger** | Issuance of Substantial Completion Certificate (target: January 31, 2027) |

**Expected reasoning:** The Commissioning obligation is distinct from the EPC Works because it results in a specific, contractually defined output — a tested, certified facility — and revenue is recognised only when the Owner's Independent Engineer issues the Substantial Completion Certificate. At that moment, control of the commissioned facility transfers to the Owner. Until the Certificate is issued, the Contractor bears the risk of Performance Test failure and remediation. This is a point-in-time event under ASC 606-10-25-30. The obligation does not meet any of the over-time criteria: the Contractor could theoretically commission a similar facility for another owner (alternative use exists for the commissioning service itself), and no incremental payment right exists until the Certificate is issued.

**Note for graders:** This is the hardest obligation in the assignment. Strong students will distinguish the commissioning service from the construction works and correctly identify that the Certificate is the control-transfer event — not the physical completion of construction. Students who classify commissioning as over-time (cost-to-cost or time-elapsed) should be asked to identify which over-time criterion is met; none are clearly satisfied.

## Discussion Points for Class

**1. Why is commissioning a separate obligation?**
The commissioning service is distinct because: (a) the Owner could theoretically hire a different party to commission a facility that Meridian constructed; (b) the Performance Tests are a specific deliverable with defined pass/fail criteria; and (c) the contract separately prices it at USD 4,000,000 consistent with observable SSPs. Distinctness under ASC 606-10-25-19 requires both distinct in isolation and distinct within the context of the contract.

**2. Does the mobilisation advance affect revenue recognition?**
No. The advance is a financing arrangement — it is recovered ratably over the first 6 months via deduction from Progress Certificates. It does not represent payment for a separate performance obligation. Revenue is still recognized based on the cost-to-cost method applied to the EPC Works price.

**3. Does retention affect revenue recognition?**
No. Retention (5% withheld from each certificate) affects the timing of cash collection, not the pattern of revenue recognition. Revenue is recognized when performance occurs, not when cash is received.

**4. What happens if Substantial Completion is delayed?**
Liquidated damages (Article 7.1) reduce the net consideration received but do not change the recognition pattern. The USD 4,000,000 commissioning fee may need to be adjusted if LDs are deducted, but the point-in-time recognition trigger (the Certificate) remains unchanged.

## Grader Notes

- The EPC contract is harder than the SaaS contract by design. Do not expect every student to correctly identify commissioning as point-in-time. A student who argues over-time for commissioning with a coherent reasoning chain that they can defend in `reasoning.md` may still receive partial marks.
- The key differentiator between strong and weak submissions on this contract is whether the student can articulate *why* commissioning is a separate obligation — not just that it has a separate price tag.
- Students from a construction or infrastructure background may recognise the IAS 11 / IFRS 15 parallels; this is acceptable and should be credited.
